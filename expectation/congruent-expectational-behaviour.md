# Congruent Expectational Behaviour

## Status

Core Doctrine  
Transformer Method Canon

---

## Definition

Congruent Expectational Behaviour is the automatic alignment of thoughts, emotions, decisions, actions, and habits with what a person genuinely expects to experience.

People do not consistently behave according to what they want.

People behave according to what they expect.

Expectation drives behaviour.

Behaviour produces results.

Results reinforce expectation.

---

## Core Principle

Most people believe their behaviour creates their expectations.

The opposite is closer to the truth.

Expectation creates behaviour.

People naturally act in alignment with what they believe is likely, possible, available, and attainable.

## What Is Expectation?

Expectation is more than hope.

Expectation is more than desire.

Expectation is the internal experience of certainty.

Wish:

```text
I hope it happens.
```

Expectation:

```text
I expect it to happen.
```

People move differently when they expect success.

People move differently when they expect failure.

---

## The Experience Of

Expectation is the experience of.

People do not respond to reality.

People respond to their expectation of reality.

Two people can face the same circumstance and behave completely differently because they expect different outcomes.

---

## Identity Creates Expectation

Expectation originates in identity.

People ask:

```text
What do I expect?
```

But the deeper question is:

```text
Who do I believe I am?
```

Identity determines:

- what feels possible
- what feels realistic
- what feels available
- what feels normal

---

## Self-Image And Expectation

A person cannot consistently outperform their self-image.

If a person's self-image says:

```text
I am not worthy.
```

They will expect rejection.

If a person's self-image says:

```text
I am successful.
```

They will expect opportunity.

The expectation changes before the result changes.

---

## Behaviour Follows Expectation

Behaviour becomes automatic when expectation is strong.

People who expect:

### Rejection

Will:

- hesitate
- avoid
- delay
- overthink
- withdraw

---

### Success

Will:

- initiate
- engage
- persist
- communicate
- follow through

The behaviour appears different.

The expectation created it.

---

## The Invisible Driver

Most behaviour is not consciously chosen.

Most behaviour is the expression of expectation.

This is why people often sabotage opportunities.

Consciously they want success.

Subconsciously they expect failure.

Behaviour follows expectation.

---

## Self-Sabotage Explained

Self-sabotage is rarely a lack of desire.

Self-sabotage is usually an expectation conflict.

Example:

```text
Conscious Goal
↓
I Want Success
```

But:

```text
Subconscious Expectation
↓
Success Is Not For Me
```

This creates internal conflict.

Behaviour becomes inconsistent.

Results become inconsistent.

## Congruence

Transformation occurs when expectation becomes congruent.

Congruence means:

```text
Identity
Expectation
Thought
Emotion
Behaviour
```

all point in the same direction.

When these elements align:

Behaviour becomes natural.

Cells Of Recognition
