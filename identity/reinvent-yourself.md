# Reinvent Yourself

## Status

Core Doctrine  
Transformer Method Canon

---

## Definition

Reinventing yourself is the conscious process of creating a new identity that is capable of producing a new reality.

Most people attempt to change their circumstances.

Very few attempt to change themselves.

The Transformer Method teaches that lasting change occurs when identity changes first.

A new life requires a new version of you.

---

## Core Principle

You do not get what you want.

You get what you are congruent with.

If you want different results, you must become a different person.

Not a different human being.

A different version of yourself.

---

## The Universal Law Of Transformation

Every transformation follows the same sequence.

```text
Identity
↓
Thoughts
↓
Emotions
↓
Behaviour
↓
Results
↓
References
↓
Identity
```

If identity remains the same, results eventually return to familiar patterns.

To create lasting change:

Identity must evolve.

---

## The Reinvention Question

Most people ask:

```text
What do I want?
```

A more powerful question is:

```text
Who do I need to become?
```

This question changes everything.

It shifts focus from acquisition to transformation.

---

## Why Most People Stay The Same

Human beings naturally seek familiarity.

Even when familiar results are painful.

The subconscious mind often prefers familiar discomfort over unfamiliar possibility.

As a result:

People repeat patterns.

They repeat behaviours.

They repeat relationships.

They repeat financial outcomes.

They repeat emotional experiences.

The external pattern continues because the internal identity remains unchanged.

---

## The Prison Of The Past

Many people unconsciously allow their past to define their future.

They build identity from:

- mistakes
- failures
- disappointments
- criticism
- limitations
- old references

The result becomes:

```text
Past
↓
Identity
↓
Future
```

The future becomes a repetition of history.

---

## The Creator's Approach

Creators reverse the sequence.

```text
Vision
↓
Identity
↓
Behaviour
↓
Future
```

Instead of allowing the past to define identity, they allow vision to define identity.

---

## You Are Not Your History

Experiences influence identity.

They do not define identity.

An event is something that happened.

Identity is who you decide to become.

Confusing the two creates limitation.

---

## The Future Self Principle

Within every person exists a future version capable of producing extraordinary results.

The purpose of transformation is not to become someone else.

The purpose is to become more of who you are capable of becoming.

Ask:

```text
Who is my future self?
```

Then ask:

```text
How does that person think?

How does that person feel?

How does that person act?

What standards do they live by?

What do they tolerate?

What do they refuse to tolerate?
```

The answers reveal the path of reinvention.

---

## Identity Is A Choice

Most people treat identity as a discovery.

The Transformer Method treats identity as a creation.

Identity is not merely found.

Identity is consciously designed.

Every day you are reinforcing an identity.

The question is:

```text
Is it the identity you want?
```

---

## The Reinvention Process

### Step 1: Awareness

Become aware of the current identity.

Ask:

```text
Who am I being?
```

You cannot change what you cannot see.

---

### Step 2: Vision

Create a compelling future.

Ask:

```text
Who must I become to create this vision?
```

Vision creates direction.

---

### Step 3: Decision

Make a non-negotiable decision.

Transformation begins with commitment.

Not preference.

Decision.

---

### Step 4: Identity Creation

Design the new identity.

Define:

- beliefs
- standards
- habits
- behaviours
- expectations

of the future self.

---

### Step 5: Congruent Action

Act as the future self before complete evidence exists.

Identity grows through action.

---

### Step 6: References

Collect proof.

Every success becomes evidence.

Every reference strengthens identity.

---

### Step 7: Reinforcement

Continue until the new identity feels natural.

What was once uncomfortable becomes normal.

## The Identity Gap

Every goal contains an identity gap.

Example:

```text
Current Identity
↓
Desired Identity
```

The size of the gap determines the amount of transformation required.

The goal is rarely the challenge.

The identity gap is.

---

## Standards Create Identity

Identity expresses itself through standards.

People do not rise to their goals.

They fall to their standards.

As standards increase:

Identity expands.

As identity expands:

Results improve.

---

## The Environment Principle

Environment reinforces identity.

People, conversations, media, and habits either strengthen or weaken the person you are becoming.

Transformation accelerates when environment supports reinvention.

---

## Frequency Follows Identity

Identity generates frequency.

Frequency influences behaviour.

Behaviour creates results.

```text
Identity
↓
Frequency
↓
Behaviour
↓
Results
```

This is why changing frequency without changing identity produces temporary results.

Identity is the source.

Every reinvention begins with a new thought.

A thought that transcends current circumstances.

A thought that challenges existing limitations.

A thought that introduces a new possibility.

Without new thinking:

No new identity emerges.

Something must begin.

```text
Old Identity
↓
Release
↓
Transition

New Identity
```

Transformation is rarely addition alone.
