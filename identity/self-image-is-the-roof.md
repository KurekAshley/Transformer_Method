# Self-Image Is The Roof

## Status

Core Doctrine  
Transformer Method Canon

---

## Definition

The self-image acts as the invisible roof over every area of life.

It determines how much success, happiness, love, health, wealth, fulfillment, and achievement a person is capable of consistently experiencing.

A person may temporarily exceed their self-image.

They cannot permanently exceed it.

Eventually they return to what feels normal.

The self-image establishes the upper limits of performance.

---

## Core Principle

People do not consistently outperform their self-image.

They perform in alignment with it.

```text
Self-Image
↓
Expectation
↓
Behaviour
↓
Results
```

The self-image becomes the ceiling for results.

---

## The Roof Analogy

Imagine a room with a ceiling eight feet high.

No matter how hard you jump, you cannot permanently live above the ceiling.

You may touch it.

You may momentarily exceed it.

But you will return below it.

The same principle applies to identity.

The self-image determines what feels normal.

Life eventually returns to that level.

The invisible roof.

## Why Success Is Often Temporary

Many people experience temporary breakthroughs.

A promotion.

A business success.

Weight loss.

A new relationship.

A financial windfall.

Yet after a period of time they return to previous patterns.

The reason is simple.

The results changed.

The self-image did not.

```text
New Result
↓
Internal Conflict
↓
Self-Sabotage
↓
Return To Familiar
```

The roof remained unchanged.

## The Thermostat Principle

The self-image operates like a thermostat.

A thermostat determines the temperature of a room.

A self-image determines the level of life a person experiences.

If the thermostat is set to:

```text
22°C
```

the room eventually returns there.

Likewise:

If a person's self-image is set to:

```text
I struggle financially.
```

Life eventually returns to that expectation.

The solution is not fighting the temperature.

The solution is changing the setting.

## What Feels Normal Wins

The subconscious mind seeks familiarity.

Not necessarily success.

Not necessarily happiness.

Familiarity.

If struggle feels normal:

Success feels uncomfortable.

If scarcity feels normal:

Abundance feels uncomfortable.

If chaos feels normal:

Peace feels uncomfortable.

People often mistake familiarity for truth.
