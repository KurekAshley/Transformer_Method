# Vision Over Circumstance

## Status

Core Doctrine  
Transformer Method Canon

---

## Definition

The quality of a person's life is determined by what they focus on.

Most people focus on circumstances.

Creators focus on vision.

Circumstances represent current reality.

Vision represents future possibility.

Transformation occurs when vision becomes more influential than circumstance.

The future is created by vision, not by current conditions.

---

## Core Principle

Whatever dominates attention influences direction.

If circumstances dominate attention:

The past is repeated.

If vision dominates attention:

The future is created.

```text
Circumstance Focus
↓
Reaction
↓
Repeated Reality
```

or

```text
Vision Focus
↓
Creation
↓
New Reality
```

The choice determines the future.

## The Fundamental Difference

Reactors ask:

```text
What is?
```

Creators ask:

```text
What could be?
```

Reactors are controlled by current conditions.

Creators are guided by future possibilities.

The distinction changes everything.

## Circumstances Describe The Present

Circumstances provide information.

They do not provide direction.

Circumstances reveal where a person is.

Vision reveals where a person is going.

Many people mistakenly allow current conditions to determine future possibilities.

Creators refuse to do so.

## The Windshield Principle

When driving a car, the windshield is larger than the rear-view mirror.

The reason is simple.

The future requires more attention than the past.

Yet many people live with the opposite ratio.

They spend more time looking backward than forward.

They focus more on what has happened than on what is possible.

Vision restores proper direction.
