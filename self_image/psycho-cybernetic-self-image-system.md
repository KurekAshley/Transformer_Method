# Psycho-Cybernetic Self-Image System

## Status

Core Doctrine  
Transformer Method Canon

---

## Definition

The Psycho-Cybernetic Self-Image System explains why people consistently produce results that match their internal picture of themselves.

Every person carries an internal image of who they believe they are.

That image acts as a thermostat for performance, behaviour, success, health, relationships, wealth, and happiness.

People do not consistently outperform their self-image.

They act in alignment with it.

---

## Core Principle

The most powerful thought a person can think is:

```text
Who Am I?
```

The answer to that question determines:

- what feels possible
- what feels impossible
- what feels natural
- what feels uncomfortable
- what feels deserved
- what feels available

The self-image silently governs behaviour.

---

```text
Self-Image
↓
Expectation
↓
Behaviour
↓
Results
↓
References

```

This loop is continuously active.

Every result either strengthens or weakens the current self-image.

---

## What Is A Self-Image?

A self-image is not who you are.

A self-image is who you believe you are.

These are often very different things.

The self-image is a collection of:

- beliefs
- experiences
- interpretations
- emotional memories
- repeated thoughts
- personal conclusions

that create an identity.

---

## The Thermostat Principle

A thermostat controls temperature.

A self-image controls results.

If a thermostat is set to:

```text
22°C
```

it will continuously bring the room back to that temperature.

Likewise:

If a person's self-image is set to:

```text
"I struggle financially."
```

they will unconsciously return to that level.

Even after temporary success.

---

## Why People Self-Sabotage

People do not sabotage success because they want failure.

People sabotage success because success exceeds their self-image.

The subconscious mind seeks congruence.

When results exceed identity:

```text
Success
↓
Internal Conflict
↓
Self-Sabotage
↓
Return To Familiar
```

The person returns to what feels normal.

---

## References Build Identity

Every experience becomes a reference.

References are the evidence we use to define ourselves.

Example:

```text
Success
↓
Reference
↓
Confidence
↓
Expanded Self-Image
```

or

```text
Failure
↓
Negative Meaning
↓
Reduced Self-Image
```

The event itself is not the issue.

The meaning attached to the event becomes the issue.

---

## The Meaning Machine

Human beings are meaning-making machines.

Events have no inherent meaning.

Meaning is assigned.

Two people can experience the same event.

One becomes empowered.

One becomes disempowered.

The difference is interpretation.

---

## The Roof Principle

The self-image acts like a roof.

A person can push above the roof temporarily.

They cannot stay above it permanently.

Eventually they return to the level they believe they deserve.

To create lasting change:

The roof must be raised.
