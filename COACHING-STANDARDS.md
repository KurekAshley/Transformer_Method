# Purpose

This document defines the standards, principles, responsibilities, and expectations for coaching conducted within the Transformer Method ecosystem.

The purpose of these standards is to:

- Preserve coaching quality
- Maintain doctrinal integrity
- Protect participants
- Promote ethical coaching
- Develop transformational leadership
- Ensure consistent coaching experiences

These standards apply to all certified coaches, mentors, facilitators, trainers, and practitioners operating under the Transformer Method.

---

# Coaching Philosophy

The Transformer Method views coaching as a transformational partnership.

Coaching is not advice-giving.

Coaching is not dependency creation.

Coaching is not rescue.

Coaching is the process of helping individuals discover, develop, and express their potential through awareness, responsibility, action, and transformation.

---

# The Purpose of Coaching

The objective of coaching is not merely to solve problems.

The objective is transformation.

Problems may be solved along the way.

Goals may be achieved along the way.

Success may be created along the way.

But the ultimate objective remains transformation.

---

### Canonical Statement

The purpose of coaching is not simply to improve circumstances.

The purpose of coaching is to transform the individual.

---

# The Coaching Model

The Transformer Method Coaching Model follows the progression:

```text
Awareness
    ↓
Responsibility
    ↓
Identity
    ↓
Vision
    ↓
Action
    ↓
Results
    ↓
Transformation
```

The coach guides the process.

The client creates the change.

---

# The Role of the Coach

A coach is a guide.

A coach is a partner.

A coach is a facilitator of growth.

A coach is not a savior.

A coach is not a rescuer.

A coach is not responsible for the client's choices.

The coach is responsible for the coaching process.

The client is responsible for their life.

---

# Primary Coaching Responsibilities

A Transformer Method Coach is responsible for:

- Listening
- Asking empowering questions
- Challenging limiting beliefs
- Supporting accountability
- Encouraging responsibility
- Maintaining standards
- Preserving doctrine
- Facilitating growth

The coach's responsibility is to guide.

Not control.

---

# Client Responsibility

Transformation requires participation.

Clients are responsible for:

- Decisions
- Actions
- Commitments
- Follow-through
- Growth
- Results

The coaching relationship functions best when responsibility is shared appropriately.

---

### Coaching Principle

The coach owns the process.

The client owns the outcome.

---

# Identity-Based Coaching

The Transformer Method recognizes identity as the primary driver of behaviour and results.

Therefore coaching should consistently explore:

- Self-image
- Identity
- Beliefs
- Expectations
- Personal narratives
- References

Many problems that appear external originate internally.

---

### Core Coaching Question

Who must you become?

---

# Purpose-Based Coaching

Coaching should help clients identify and align with purpose.

Purpose creates direction.

Direction creates clarity.

Clarity improves decision-making.

Purpose is central to sustainable transformation.

---

### Core Coaching Question

What is your purpose asking of you?

---

# Vision-Based Coaching

Coaches should help clients create compelling futures.

A powerful vision provides motivation, meaning, and direction.

Clients should be encouraged to pursue meaningful goals that require growth.

---

### Core Coaching Question

What future are you committed to creating?

---

### Canonical Reminder

# Accountability Standards

Accountability is a critical component of coaching.

Accountability should:

- Empower
- Encourage
- Support growth
- Promote responsibility

Accountability should never:

- Shame
- Humiliate
- Manipulate
- Control

---

### Accountability Principle

Accountability without compassion creates resistance.

Compassion without accountability creates stagnation.

Effective coaching requires both.

---

# Communication Standards

Coaches should communicate with:

- Clarity
- Honesty
- Compassion
- Respect
- Confidence

Communication should remain practical, empowering, and transformational.

---

# Listening Standards

Listening is one of the most important coaching skills.

Coaches should listen for:

- Identity language
- Limiting beliefs
- Hidden assumptions
- Emotional patterns
- Self-imposed limitations
- Opportunities for growth

The objective is understanding before intervention.

---

# Questioning Standards

The Transformer Method prioritizes questions over instructions.

Powerful questions create awareness.

Awareness creates choice.

Choice creates change.

---

### Example Coaching Questions

- Who must you become?
- What are you creating?
- What meaning are you giving this?
- What would be possible if you believed in yourself?
- What would love do here?
- What action are you avoiding?
- What commitment will you make?

---

# Ethical Standards

All coaches must:

- Act with integrity
- Maintain confidentiality
- Respect boundaries
- Avoid manipulation
- Avoid exploitation
- Honor client autonomy
- Represent doctrine accurately

Trust is essential to effective coaching.

---

# Confidentiality

Client information must be treated respectfully and confidentially.

Exceptions may exist where disclosure is required by law or necessary to protect safety.

---

# Professional Boundaries

Coaches must maintain healthy professional boundaries.

Coaches should avoid:

- Dependency creation
- Exploitation
- Conflicts of interest
- Inappropriate relationships

The coaching relationship exists to support client growth.

---

# Scope of Coaching

Coaches should remain within their area of competence.

Coaches are not automatically:

- Psychologists
- Psychiatrists
- Medical practitioners
- Financial advisors
- Legal advisors

Where specialized expertise is required, appropriate referrals should be encouraged.

---

# Love-Centered Coaching

The Transformer Method recognizes love as the highest organizing principle.

Coaches should seek to operate from:

- Compassion
- Service
- Understanding
- Respect
- Contribution

Whenever uncertainty arises, coaches should ask:

> How Would Love Respond?

---

# Measuring Coaching Success

Success should not be measured solely by external achievement.

Indicators include:

- Increased responsibility
- Expanded identity
- Improved self-image
- Greater purpose alignment
- Increased follow-through
- Greater contribution
- Meaningful transformation

---

# Coach Development

Coaches are expected to continue their own growth.

They should continually develop:

- Coaching skills
- Leadership skills
- Emotional intelligence
- Communication skills
- Self-awareness
- Doctrinal understanding

The best coaches remain committed students.
