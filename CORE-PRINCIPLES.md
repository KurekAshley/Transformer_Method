# Purpose

This document defines the fundamental principles upon which the Transformer Method is built.

These principles represent the foundational truths that govern all doctrines, models, frameworks, distinctions, and applications within the Transformer Method Canon.

While individual doctrines may expand upon these principles, they may never contradict them.

These principles serve as the philosophical and practical foundation of the entire system.

---

# What Is a Core Principle?

A core principle is a foundational truth that remains consistent across circumstances, environments, industries, cultures, and life situations.

Core principles are not techniques.

They are not strategies.

They are not trends.

They are enduring truths that govern human transformation and performance.

---

# Core Principle 1

## Human Beings Can Change

Transformation is possible.

No person is permanently limited by their past, current circumstances, conditioning, failures, or experiences.

People can learn.

People can grow.

People can evolve.

The capacity for transformation exists within every individual.

---

### Canonical Statement

Human beings possess the ability to transform themselves and their lives through conscious growth and intentional action.

# Core Principle 2

## Identity Creates Reality

The life a person experiences is heavily influenced by who they believe themselves to be.

Identity influences:

- Decisions
- Behaviour
- Performance
- Relationships

- Results

Sustainable transformation occurs when identity changes.

# Core Principle 3

## The Self-Image Is the Roof

Every person lives beneath an internal ceiling created by their self-image.

A person may temporarily exceed their self-image, but long-term results tend to return to the level of identity they believe they deserve.

To create a larger life, the self-image must expand.

---

### Canonical Statement

The self-image determines the level of success, happiness, abundance, and fulfillment a person is willing to accept.
