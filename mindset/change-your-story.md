# Change Your Story

## Status

Core Doctrine  
Transformer Method Canon

---

## Definition

The story you tell yourself becomes the life you experience.

Every person has a story.

A story about:

- who they are
- what is possible
- what they deserve
- what life means
- what happened in their past
- what their future will be

Most people are not living their life.

They are living their story about life.

Transformation begins when the story changes.

---

## Core Principle

Events do not determine destiny.

Meaning determines destiny.

```text
Event
↓
Meaning
↓
Story
↓
Identity
↓
Behaviour
↓
Results
```

The event may be in the past.

The story continues in the present.

---

## The Great Illusion

Most people believe:

```text
My life created my story.
```

In reality:

```text
My story is creating my life.
```

The story determines:

- what is noticed
- what is ignored
- what is expected
- what is attempted
- what is avoided

The story becomes the filter through which reality is interpreted.

---

## Events Have No Meaning

Events happen.

Meaning is assigned.

Two people can experience the same event and create completely different lives.

```text
Event
↓
Meaning
↓
Future
```

The event is identical.

The meaning is different.

Therefore the future becomes different.

---

## The Meaning Machine

Human beings are meaning-making creatures.

Every experience is interpreted.

Every interpretation becomes a story.

Every story becomes a belief.

Every belief becomes a lens.

That lens influences every future decision.

---

## The Identity Story

Most identity is constructed from stories.

```text
Story
↓
Belief
↓
Identity
```

Examples:

```text
I'm not good enough.

People always leave me.

Money is difficult.

Success is for other people.

I always fail.

Life is a struggle.
```

These are not facts.

They are stories.

Repeated stories eventually become identities.

---

## The Victim Story

One of the most destructive stories is victimhood.

The victim story says:

```text
Life is happening to me.
```

The victim focuses on:

- blame
- unfairness
- circumstances
- limitations

The story removes personal power.

---

## The Creator Story

The Creator asks:

```text
What does this mean?

What can I learn?

How can I grow?

How can I use this?
```

The Creator understands:

Meaning is a choice.

The story can be rewritten.

Power returns the moment responsibility returns.

---

## The Reference Cycle

Stories create references.

References create identity.

Identity creates behaviour.

```text
Story
↓
Reference
↓
Identity
↓
Behaviour
↓
Results
↓
Story
```

The cycle continues until the story changes.

---

## The Prison Of Interpretation

Most people are imprisoned by interpretations made years earlier.

An event occurs.

A conclusion is drawn.

The conclusion becomes permanent.

For example:

```text
Business failed.
```

Meaning assigned:

```text
I'm not good at business.
```

Identity formed:

```text
I'm not an entrepreneur.
```

Future created:

Avoidance of opportunity.

The event never created the limitation.

The interpretation did.

---

## The Empowering Question

Every story originates from a question.

Disempowering questions create disempowering stories.

Examples:

```text
Why does this always happen to me?

Why am I unlucky?

Why am I not enough?
```

Empowering questions create empowering stories.

Examples:

```text
What can I learn?

How can this serve me?

How can I use this?

What is the opportunity here?
```

Questions determine meaning.

Meaning determines stories.

Stories determine lives.

---

## The Power Of Reframing

Reframing means assigning a new meaning to an old event.

The event remains unchanged.

The interpretation changes.

```text
Old Event
↓
New Meaning
↓
New Story
↓
New Future
```

Reframing does not deny reality.

Reframing changes the relationship with reality.

---

## The Hero's Journey

Every hero experiences adversity.

The difference is not the challenge.

The difference is the story.

The victim says:

```text
This is the end.
```

The hero says:

```text
This is part of the journey.
```

One story creates defeat.

The other creates growth.

---

## The Story Of Failure

Most people misunderstand failure.

Failure is not an identity.

Failure is an event.

Yet many people convert an event into an identity.

```text
I failed.
```

becomes:

```text
I am a failure.
```

This is one of the most damaging story shifts possible.

Events should never become identities.

---

## The Story Of Success

Successful people tell different stories.

They do not deny setbacks.

They interpret them differently.

They ask:

```text
What is this teaching me?
```

The story becomes:

```text
Everything is preparing me.
```

This creates resilience.

---

## The Future Story

Every person is living toward a story.

Some stories create limitation.

Others create expansion.

Examples:

```text
I'm too old.

I'm too young.

It's too late.

I'm not qualified.
```

These stories create predictable futures.

New stories create new futures.

Transformation requires a new story.

The old story created the old life.

A new life requires a new interpretation.

```text
New Story
↓
New Identity
↓
New Behaviour
↓
New Results
```

The future changes when the story changes.

Stories generate emotions.

Emotions generate frequency.

Frequency influences attraction.

```text

↓
Emotion
↓
Frequency

Recognition
↓
Behaviour
↓
```

## The Responsibility Shift

The most powerful moment in transformation occurs when a person realizes:

```text
I am not my story.
```

The story was learned.

The story can be changed.

The story can be upgraded.

The story can be rewritten.

## The Author Principle

Most people are characters in their story.

Creators become authors.

Characters react.

Authors create.

The moment a person becomes the author, transformation begins.

### What evidence contradicts it?

The answers reveal the stories directing behaviour.
